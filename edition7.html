Chosen Fate

By Bhargavi

Welcome to the game of choice.

Where you get to pick the way our character’s life goes.

This goes without saying, but try not to kill them please.



I’m locked inside this place… I have no memory of how I got here. I don’t think there’s anyone else in here with me, or maybe there are people asleep like I was behind the locked doors I found. I wonder why only the door to my room was left open… did they know I would wake up first?

It’s stupid to ask myself, I know, but who even is the ‘they’ I’m referring to? Who, or what put me here? Was what brought me here even human? Why am I here? I have so many questions.

I’ve walked around the place I seem to be stuck in. There are 5 rooms, including the one I found myself in. All the other rooms were locked… I don’t even want to know what waits behind the closed doors. There’s a kitchen, with a stove that won’t go out. It’s a small stove, won’t cause much harm, but it’s simply not going out no matter how much I try. There seems to be a strange key-shaped hole in it… I wonder what that means. There’s a fridge that doesn’t work, and a horrible stench of rotten food wafts out when I open it. Or maybe something else is causing the smell. I refuse to hold it open long enough to find out.

There aren’t any bathrooms in sight. Guess we’ll cross that bridge when we get there.

Despite how much this place looks like a house, I don’t see a front door, or something that will lead me out. I’ve looked all over… the windows are nailed shut. There’s no visible way out.

I see a staircase that I didn’t see before. I’m sure I walked around the whole place. There’s no way I could have missed a giant, grand looking stairway to downstairs. This only proves my theories.

I’m locked in some kind of paranormal, fantastical place.

Or I’m hallucinating. Big time.

After an internal debate, I take the stairway down. There are no lights here… or windows. Boy, I wouldn’t know if it was day or night in this place. I can walk down a stairway I apparently imagined. This makes it clear that it’s no hallucination.

This place seems to be a long passageway. Fire-lit torches pop up along as I walk, right at my pace and thankfully illuminating my path so I’m not walking into pitch-black air. When I reach the supposed end, I’m standing face to face with a stone wall.

Well, that was disappointing. I hoped to find some kind of clue as to where I was, who brought me here, anything, but all I see is a stone wall. I turn around to walk back down the path and up the stairs to the house I was in. In a last-ditch attempt, I turn around to see if I’ve missed anything.

To my immense surprise, the stone wall… vanished. In its place, there is a note with tiny handwriting on it, floating down from the ceiling, like someone just dropped it. Of course, I walk back to it, and pick it up to read it. It says

“As soon as you finish reading this note, it will transform into something that will help you leave this place. I originally had no intention of letting you out, but seeing as you’ve made it all the way here, I suppose I must abide by my own rules and allow you to leave. If you thought it would be easy, you are hugely mistaken. I have only one thing left to say now. Be careful with what you will hold in seconds, it has the power to bring forth a vicious, bloodthirsty enemy and a helpful ally. Make the right choice, or take your chances with death.”

Right as I read the word ‘death’, the paper burns away with an invisible flame, and leaves a key in its place.

Could this be… a key to one of the doors upstairs?

I could try opening them! Yes, finally, someone to keep me company in this strange place- wait. What if… what if what the note said is real, and an enemy of mine waits behind a door? How am I to know what’s behind which door? I don’t know what to do!

The Chronicles Of Joy - Chapter 4: Fortune

By Sriram

Y’know, in most cases, telling the king of the universe that the queen of the universe has died isn’t a good thing. The king is most likely to chase you screaming, ‘YOU LET MY WIFE DIE, FOOL! I WILL FIND YOU AND PERSONALLY KILL YOU, BRING YOU BACK ALIVE AND KILL YOU AGAIN!’

Fortune was no exception. So here was I, running while screaming at the top of my lungs. He was intimidating. He had a very sharp sword. I stopped running, brandished my sword and faced him, trying to resolve my problems by talking. ‘Listen, brother-in-law. The plan for killing Evil was Luck’s idea, not mine. The fault that she died under Evil’s stroke also had nothing to do with me. You’re chasing me with no real reason.’ Fortune became very still and unmoving. Suddenly, he fell right into my hands and hugged me. ‘The-TheThe only- The only wife I had. I lost her- I lost her. The-The blame is not yours, brother- It’s- It’s mine.’ He straightened up and brushed his tears, making up his resolve.

‘I’d bring my wife back, Evil- Watch out, mother. You always said I was a good swordsman, and gifted me with my own sword. This sword shall be the end of you.’

‘Whoa. Is that for sure? You-You know that for sure? Asking ‘cause that can grant me a lot of peace.’

‘I know the outcome, brother. But I also know that sharing is not in my place to do it. The outcome will be affected by that.’

My thoughts were racing. If he cared so much about changing the outcome, it had to be a good outcome, right? Because if he went worrying about bad outcomes changing into good ones, he’d lose his street cred. Inwardly, I grinned to myself. I was a hundred percent sure that we’d end up beating Evil in the end.

‘Brother. Let’s go. Moral and Happiness are here. Now, where did you say Evil’s new domain was?’

‘In the Big Apple in New York.’

‘Let’s go. Moral, you ready?’

Dad came up grinning, waving his stick. ‘Happiness?’ Mom bared her claws.

‘Losing Luck has impacted us all, and I can easily feel the grief and rage. Let’s pummel that grief and rage on Evil, shall we, now?’ Mom said. Cheering, we all went down from the heavens into New York.

‘Whoa. Look at the Big Apple now, Happiness! It’s beautiful. Remember the day when-’

‘Dad.’

‘Sorry, son. What’s Evil’s door number, now?’

‘I don’t remember all that. All I know is that it’s green with a red finish on the roof.’ Fortune soared up into the air, searching the streets for a house that matched my description. ‘Dang, that guy can fly? I checked Amazon and IKEA and Flipkart for wings like that, but I couldn’t- Oh.’

I stopped with a sheepish look on my face. What do you think? I tell my Dad not to distract us and get distracted myself? ‘So, Fortune. And speaking of him, where’s he?’ As if on cue, Fortune landed gently next to us. ‘I found her house, easy. She’s weaponized it. That’s the problem right here. We can probably get through them in our goddy avatars, but it wouldn’t be as easy as-’

‘“-Bringing Chaos to solve the problem for us.”’ My dad completed Fortune’s sentence for him and grinned. ‘So off to Chaos, isn’t it? What are we waiting for?’ Saying this, he and Fortune soared into the air. It was a solid hundred meters before they remembered that me and Mom couldn’t fly and came back. ‘So, who’s Chaos?’

‘Your younger brother. He has a reputation for making things screw up. Whenever he’s around, machines break down, cars’ breaks don’t work and I always lose Game of Thrones,’ said Dad.

‘You what, now?’

‘Forget it.’ Mom, Fortune and I suppressed giggles. We were soaring high above Brooklyn when Dad asked, ‘Where’s Chaos’ place, by the way, Fortune?’

‘Up north, Moral - He lives where the giants live so that he can wreck their evil plans.’ ‘Chaos put to good use.’ Then they both grinned like maniacs. And they were no better the whole way to Yukon, Canada, where Chaos lived.

-

It didn’t go as it was planned. Enter Alatar the Blue and Strength. As we were nearing Yukon, we met rivals in air. The guys were tough. Strength was, like, invulnerable. Moral would turn to fight her and run away screaming. She’d read our minds, and just as I’d block a backhand she’d pull my sword away.

And Alatar- the guy had mad power. He manipulated time. Like, there was this one time where I swung at him. Calm. He clicked a finger on his staff and just like that- everyone stopped moving, but we could see. He skipped away, shrieking, ‘Tra-La-La! I can’t hear you!’ And every time Alatar lost focus and got wounded, Strength would rejuvenate him.

We barely managed to escape them. And even that was not because of our bravery or anything- It was because when I cut of the head of Alatar, Moral kept Strength busy enough (By pouring pepper in her eyes) to ensure that Alatar died.

He didn’t die.

At the last second, Moral let go of Strength and we all ran for our lives. They escaped, and we all lived happily ever after for about twenty minutes.

Then came Atheris, Mistress of Snakes. If she had wanted to, she could have killed us and lived happily ever after. But nope. She didn’t, which was a surprise. She turned us away when we asked for supplies. When we hung around planning in her yard, she sent me a message.

Surprise, surprise, we decided to leave. Then, we had to deal with Rebel. That girl was tough prey. I was like, when do we get easy prey? The girl did nothing by the usual way. She was a leftie with her sword. She kept her hair in two space buns. She wore her shirt backwards to front. In short, she was like a teen rebel, and her nature suited her name perfectly. Which also meant trouble for us.

The usual ways of maiming, killing, torturing and dismembering did not work with her. Every time she dodged our swipes at her, she said, ‘Punks. You guys wouldn’t know Fight if she hit you in the face.’ Yeah, she wasn’t exactly the ‘Barbie and Ponies and Rainbows’ girl. More like the ‘Fight and Fight and Fight’ girl. We had a hard, hard time getting away from her. Stretchy Symbolism!

By Vedika

Rubber bands- we use them every day, yet have we even given a thought to how symbolic they are?

They are only looked upon as functional. So let’s take a minute here, and actually think of a rubber band.

The multi-hued, thin elastic band has in its power to serve a wide range of purposes. Whether it’s holding a mop of hair up, using them in the kitchen to close pouches of snacks, they always come in handy. But my moment of epiphany came when I realised the hardy stretch mate, has a lot of life lessons to offer.

Like a rubber band, we should be able to stretch and challenge ourselves as much as we can, and bounce back every time. Sure they wear out but humans do too. The effort matters. Here are a few incredible things I noticed about rubber bands.

Acting from personal power:

Like a rubber band, we need to act from our own power, take control of our situation and hold ourselves together. We need to take action, but first we must forget all our fears.

Always stretching and challenging ourselves:

We should always strive to be the best versions of ourselves, and always push ourselves to achieve our goals. It is essential to grow and develop at every stage. The first step should be out of our comfort zone, and then further on to realizing what we’re actually capable of. It’s not our ability or inability, as the case may be, that holds us back, but the fears in our mind. But we should be realistic and know our limits. If we push too hard we may snap from the amount of pressure.

Knowing our purpose:

No one is ever insignificant or useless. If we’ve learnt anything from rubber bands it’s that we always have a purpose no matter how small it is. Once we set a goal or a target we, we have a better motivation to see things clearly, so we can push ahead past the adversities. Knowing your purpose will drive you to do better in life.

Having good reflexes:

Ever noticed how when we stretch a rubber band and let go, it immediately snaps back into shape? We should too. We need to think and act fast. Nothing should ever be able to break us, as we bounce back each time quicker than before. Having good reflexes helps with sports and most definitely requires practice. They also help keep us safer. If we have fast reflexes we’ll be able to avoid risky situations, whether physical or mental.

Accepting ourselves:

Rubber bands, like humans, come in all different shapes, colours and sizes and can be fashionable and blingy or just plain and simple to fit in with the circumstance. They too have different names and different uses- for example, a hair tie is used to hold our hair up, and elastic bands are used to grip hold things together. There is no place for superiority complex because every variety is useful in its own way. What we need to understand is that no matter what anyone says we’ll always be strong enough to believe in ourselves & stretch past demotivating words and achieve our goals.

Taking responsibility:

Like rubber bands do, we must help out wherever we can. Everything that happens in our life is a result of our actions whether they’re physical or mental. If we ever encounter a problem, no one else will be able to solve it but ourselves. We take responsibility once we accept our thoughts that are coming from our mind. The words we speak should come from within is and the actions we do should be our own.

By Aadhav

This dates back many years ago, in Pripyat (present day Ukraine); when a nuclear reactor in sector 4 exploded, causing large amounts of radioactive material such as Uranium to be hurled into the atmosphere. If you might not have guessed, this article is about Chernobyl or specifically the Chernobyl nuclear accident. This took place on April 26th 1986 when Uranium was launched into the sky due to reactor design flaws, serious breach in protocol and inexperienced group of team members. The explosion triggered a large fireball that destroyed the entire reactor and the entire power unit. The Soviet Union tried to cover the incident up and make excuses, however Sweden brought it forward. However there is more to the tale and positives to take away. Seventy Percent of the trees have grown back and the forests have been able reach their fullest. By clearing out the humans, Chernobyl has been able to retrieve all vanished unrecalled wildlife and leopard, foxes and the once extinct Przewalski’s horse are found in abundance. Chernobyl now holds the largest wolves population and Timberwolves thrive here as the tertiary predator of the food chain. Even by destroying the forests they have been able to grow back and various ecosystems exist now. This disaster has another fallout; it hastened the end of the Soviet Union which led to formation of different countries. Due to the barbarous side effects of this accident there was an anti-nuclear movement led by various different countries.

Today, Chernobyl beckons to tourists who are intrigued by its history and its hazards.

Optimism or Pessimism?

Anonymous

We tend to overestimate the probability of positive events happening in the future while grossly underestimating the probability of negative ones. Chances are when you imagine your future it is usually better than your current situation.

Pessimists are better equipped for dealing with stress and therefore perform better. Setting low expectations reduces the pressure to be perfect and encourages pessimists to think through all the possible pitfalls and thus be better prepared for setbacks.

Studies show that optimism gives you a better outlook on life, better resources for coping, and better problem-solving abilities. However, excessive optimism can lead people to overestimate the likelihood that they can experience good things while avoiding bad things. The optimism bias suggests that people often underestimate their risk of experiencing negative outcomes, and when people are overly optimistic about something, they may be less likely to think about all of the potential risks and take steps to mitigate those issues. Which could ultimately make it more likely that their efforts fail, or at least run into major problems along the way.

To be entirely optimistic about the rules always working in our favour would be stupid, but to be pessimistic about the game as a whole just because the rules don't always work in our favour would be equally as stupid. What game is a game at all where the rules always work out in the player's favour? To give up on life entirely would be like throwing out a game because we sometimes lose as if the game would be worth playing if we knew we would win every time.

Ultimately, the best approach is probably to be pessimistic about life's conditions in order to face their realities and be optimistic in our abilities to face the realities to form meaning and experience through them. Rather than being optimistic about the possibility of finding ideas that will rid ourselves and our lives of disorder and confusion, maybe we should attempt to be optimistic about the potential value found in accepting and enduring it.
